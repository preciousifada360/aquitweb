About
When you sign up an ally, the person you designate will receive an email invitation that includes information about what being an ally means. And by the way, Acquits allies are someone in your real life (not just online). While it would be nice to get all our accountability online, there are many reasons why connecting in real life can be powerful. We encourage you to talk with an ally directly prior to receiving the email. As long as that ally is on board, they will begin having access to details about you progress, at whatever level you determine. Aside from some initial guidance and support from us at the beginning, your ally will be independent. You and your ally can work out the relationship that feels best.
FAQ
Can Anyone Be An Ally?
As long as they’re providing the kind of support you need! Our experience and observation is that not everyone is willing or able to provide truly helpful support as an ally. So be smart about who you pick!
It Feels A Little Scary To Ask Someone To Help Me Like This. Any Thoughts For Me?
Join the club! Our surveys show that high percentages of Acquits users feel similar fears or worries. Opening up and being vulnerable is inherently stretching for many of us. All we can say is that it’s worth it! We can’t tell you how many times someone told us that getting accountable was a crucial part of finding lasting freedom. Maybe it will be for you too.
What If I Don’t Want My Ally To See Everything On My Dashboard?
You get to decide what the person does or doesn’t see.
How Often Will My Ally Get A Report On How I’m Doing?
That’s also up to you and your ally to decide – with customizable options available.
Can I Ask Someone Else In Acquits To Be My Ally?
Unfortunately no. There are important legal and liability reasons why we cannot facilitate the direct sharing of contact information on the website (basically, it could be abused or taken advantage of big-time…otherwise, we probably would!). While online accountability has the advantage of being more private, there is a power to the in-person connection that it also misses. So we are also legitimately interested in encouraging people to re-connect with “IRL” relationships, as one Acquits participant called them. If it takes time to warm up to that, it’s okay! All that being said, seeking and sharing support on the forum is totally fine. And this can include accountability. There are lots of threads on the forum where that can happen.
